Churrasco

Description

This dish can be prepared in a myriad of ways. Latinos from many different countries cook this dish, but this recipe will be cooked in the Cuban style. Ideally, skirt steak or flank steak is used.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs of skirt or flank steak
  • 1 large lavel orange, juiced (or 1/2 cup of sour orange)
  • 3 limes, juiced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 6 cloves garlic, finely minced
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup fresh chopped cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon coarse salt for finishing
  • Lime wedges for serving

Steps

  1. Cut steaks into about 3 or 4 smaller pieces so it's easier to fit into the marinade.
  2. Whisk orange juice/sour orange, lime juice, olive oil, garlic, salt, cumin, pepper, oregano, and cayenne pepper together in a large bowl. Place steak in 1 at a time into the marinade to thoroughly coat them. Add sliced onions and toss with the meat.
  3. Transfer mixture and marinade to a resealable plastic bag. Squeeze out air, seal bad, and place into a dish. Refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours.
  4. Heat cast iron pan to high heat and place a small amount of butter. Place steaks on cast iron pan once hot and cook for 45 seconds on each side, or until steaks have a nice sear on the outside.
  5. Place steak into oven at 225-250 degrees until internal temperature reaches 135 degrees for a medium cook. Let meat rest for about 10 minutes; the internal temperature will raise to about 145 degrees and will be ready to eat.
